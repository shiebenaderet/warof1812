## Game Overview â€” What Students Will Do

### Core Gameplay Loop (12 rounds = 1812-1815)
Students play through **5 phases each round** (~3-4 minutes per round):

1. **Event** â€” Historical event card appears with a knowledge check question
2. **Allocate** â€” Deploy reinforcement troops to territories
3. **Battle** â€” Attack adjacent enemy territories (Risk-style dice combat)
4. **Maneuver** â€” Reposition troops between friendly territories
5. **Score** â€” Victory points calculated based on territory control

### Victory Condition
- **First to 50 points wins** (progress bar always visible at top of screen)
- OR last faction standing (eliminate all opponents)

### Key Mechanics
- **Knowledge checks:** 9 required questions ensure core content mastery. Correct answers give bonus troops or nationalism points.
- **Auto-save:** Game state saves every 30-60 seconds. Students can close browser and resume later.
- **Turn journal:** Documents major events, battles, and decisions for review/discussion.
- **Multiple perspectives:** Factions have unique mechanics (U.S. nationalism meter, British naval superiority, Native guerrilla tactics).

## Facilitation Tips â€” Teacher Moves During Gameplay

### First 10 Minutes (Rounds 1-3)
- **Circulate:** Check that all students successfully launched the game
- **Answer questions:** "How do I attack?" "What does nationalism do?" (See FAQ below)
- **Highlight:** Point out the victory progress bar at top of screen
- **Troubleshoot:** Help students who can't find territories or are stuck

### Middle 20 Minutes (Rounds 4-8)
- **Monitor engagement:** Listen for strategy talk ("I need to control the Great Lakes!")
- **Spotlight learning:** "Who just answered a question about Tecumseh? What did you learn?"
- **Encourage reading:** Remind students to read event card descriptions, not just answer questions
- **Track progress:** Note which students are ahead/behind for differentiation

### Final 15 Minutes (Rounds 9-12)
- **Prep for discussion:** Identify 2-3 journal entries to discuss as a class
- **Celebrate wins:** "Who just hit 50 points? What was your winning strategy?"
- **Extend thinking:** Ask students to predict how the war will end based on their gameplay
- **Export data:** Remind students to submit scores before closing (auto-prompts at game end)

### Discussion Prompts (Use Journal Entries)
- "Your journal shows the U.S. invaded Canada. Why did that happen historically?"
- "You lost control of Washington D.C. How did that affect American nationalism in the game? In real history?"
- "Playing as the Native Coalition, what challenges did you face? How does this reflect historical reality?"

## Common Student Questions

**Q: "How do I win?"**
A: Reach 50 victory points first (check the progress bar at top) OR eliminate all opponents.

**Q: "How do I get more troops?"**
A: Each round you get reinforcements (3 + half your territory count). Control more territories = more troops. Correct knowledge check answers also give bonus troops.

**Q: "How do I attack?"**
A: In the **Battle** phase, click one of your territories, then click an adjacent enemy territory. You'll roll dice (Risk-style). More troops = better odds.

**Q: "What does nationalism do?" (U.S. faction only)**
A: Nationalism boosts your victory points each round. Build it by controlling key territories and answering questions correctly.

**Q: "Why can't I attack this territory?"**
A: You can only attack **adjacent** territories (connected by borders). Also, some territories have forts (+2 defense bonus).

**Q: "I accidentally closed the browser! Is my game lost?"**
A: No! The game auto-saves every 30-60 seconds. Reload the page and click "Restore Last Save."

**Q: "What faction should I choose?"**
A: All factions are balanced. Choose based on interest: U.S. (nationalism strategy), British (naval power), Native (early-game guerrilla tactics).

**Q: "How long is one game?"**
A: 12 rounds = full game. Most students complete 8-12 rounds in a 45-minute period. You can save and resume next class.

### Formative Assessment (During Gameplay)
- **Check for understanding:** "Why did you choose the U.S. faction? What's your strategy?"
- **Error analysis:** "You lost that battle. What could you have done differently?"

- **Journal reflection:** "Review your turn journal. What were the 3 most important events in your game? Why?"
- **Perspective essay:** "You played as [faction]. Write from the viewpoint of a soldier/leader explaining why you're fighting."
- **Counterfactual analysis:** "What if the Native Coalition had won? How would North America be different today?"
- **Class discussion:** Compare gameplay experiences across factions. What patterns emerge?

### Grading Rubric (Suggested)
- **Participation:** Completed 8+ rounds (40%)
- **Knowledge checks:** 70%+ correct on required questions (30%)
- **Reflection:** Thoughtful journal analysis or perspective writing (30%)

## Troubleshooting â€” Quick Fixes

| **Problem** | **Solution** |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Game won't load** | Check internet connection. Try refreshing page. Verify URL is correct. |
| **Stuck on intro screen** | Student must click "Continue" after reading faction description. |
| **Can't find a territory** | Zoom out (scroll wheel or pinch). Map uses real geographic coordinates â€” territories are labeled. |
| **"I can't allocate troops"** | You can only place troops on **your own territories** during Allocate phase. Click green "Continue" when done. |
| **Battle animations frozen** | Refresh page. Auto-save will restore progress (only loses last ~30 seconds). |
| **Knowledge check won't advance** | Student must select an answer and click "Submit." If stuck, refresh page. |
| **Victory progress bar not showing** | It's at the very top of the screen (thin colored bar). Zoom out if needed. |
| **Can't submit final score** | Requires internet connection. If offline, have student export save file (âš™ Menu â†’ Export Save) and resubmit later. |
| **Teacher Dashboard shows no data** | Scores only appear after students complete games and submit. Make sure students entered your class code. |

### Reset Instructions (Nuclear Option)
If a student's game is hopelessly broken:
1. Open browser console (F12)
2. Type: `localStorage.clear()`
3. Refresh page
4. **Warning:** This deletes ALL saved games. Use only as last resort.
